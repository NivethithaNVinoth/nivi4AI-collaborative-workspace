# Competitive Analysis

Competitive analysis reveals not just what others build, but how they position, who they target, and where they are weak. The output drives positioning decisions, roadmap prioritisation, and go-to-market strategy.

## When to Use
- Before defining a new product or feature area
- When a competitor launches something significant
- During annual or quarterly strategy planning
- When a prospect or customer mentions a competitor
- Before a funding round or board presentation

## Framework: Competitive Analysis (5 Steps)

### Step 1: Scope the competitive landscape
Identify three categories:
- **Direct competitors**: Same product category, same target user (3-5 companies)
- **Indirect competitors**: Different product, same job-to-be-done (2-3)
- **Potential entrants**: Large players who could enter your space (1-2)

For each, note: company stage, funding, team size (signal of investment level).

### Step 3: Positioning Map

Plot each competitor and your product. Identify:
- Clusters: where are multiple players competing head-to-head?
- Gaps: quadrants with no strong player (whitespace opportunity)
- Outliers: players pursuing a differentiated position

### Step 4: Competitor Profiles
For each direct competitor, produce a one-page profile:

**[Competitor Name]**
- **Positioning**: How they describe themselves (pull from their website/ads)
- **Target segment**: Who they primarily sell to
- **Pricing**: Model (per seat / usage / flat) and rough price range
- **Key strengths**: Top 2-3 genuine advantages
- **Key weaknesses**: Top 2-3 real gaps (from reviews, user feedback, missing features)
- **Recent moves**: Product launches, pricing changes, acquisitions in last 12 months
- **Win/loss pattern**: When do customers choose them over us, and vice versa?

### Step 5: Strategic Implications
Synthesise into actionable outputs:

**Our differentiated position:**
Where do we win that no competitor wins? (your defensible turf)

**Table stakes gaps (must close):**
Capabilities competitors have that customers expect â€” our absence here is a deal-breaker.

**Whitespace opportunities:**
Gaps in the market no competitor is addressing well.

**Strategic risks:**
Where could a competitor catch up to or leap-frog our advantage?

**Recommended moves (top 3):**
Specific actions the team should take in the next 90 days based on this analysis.

## Output Format
Generate a complete report with: landscape overview, feature matrix, positioning map summary, top-3 competitor profiles, and strategic recommendations. Total reading time should be under 10 minutes.

## Quality Checklist
- [ ] Direct AND indirect competitors are covered
- [ ] Feature matrix focuses on customer-evaluated dimensions, not internal priorities
- [ ] Strengths and weaknesses are based on evidence (reviews, customer feedback, demos) â€” not assumption
- [ ] Positioning axes reflect what customers actually use to choose between products
- [ ] Whitespace is specific and actionable, not vague ("no one does AI well")
- [ ] Recommendations are concrete with a time horizon

## Common Pitfalls
- Only analysing direct competitors â€” users always have alternatives, including doing nothing
- Feature matrix as a laundry list â€” include only features that affect buying decisions
- Listing weaknesses without evidence â€” back every claim with a source or customer quote
- Analysis without recommendations â€” research without a point of view is not useful
- Stale data â€” competitive landscapes shift fast; note when each data point was collected
